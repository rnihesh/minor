Abstract
● The project aims to develop an automatic emotion recognition system from
speech signals using deep learning techniques.
● A hybrid CNN–LSTM architecture is employed to capture both spatial and
temporal features from the audio data.
● Mel Frequency Cepstral Coefficients (MFCCs) are extracted from speech
signals and the model is trained using the RAVDESS emotional speech
dataset.
● The system classifies emotions such as happy, sad, angry, calm, and neutral,
supporting automated and non-intrusive mental health monitoring.
Introduction
• Human emotions play an important role in communication and are often
reflected in speech through variations in tone, pitch, and intensity.
• Speech Emotion Recognition (SER) is a field that focuses on automatically
identifying emotions from speech signals using computational techniques.
• With the advancement of Artificial Intelligence and Deep Learning, it has
become possible to analyze speech patterns and detect emotional states
more accurately.
• This project aims to develop an automated emotion recognition system using a
hybrid CNN–LSTM model to classify emotions from speech signals, which can
support applications such as mental health monitoring and human–computer
interaction.

Existing System Drawbacks
• Manual emotion assessment is subjective and depends on human
interpretation.
• Traditional systems rely on basic machine learning methods, which have limited
accuracy in detecting emotions.
• Many existing approaches fail to capture temporal patterns in speech signals
effectively.
• The systems are not scalable and require significant human effort for analysis.
